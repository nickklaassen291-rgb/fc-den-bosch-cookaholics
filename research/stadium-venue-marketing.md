# Stadium as Event Venue: Research Report for Cookaholics × FC Den Bosch

*Research date: February 2026*

---

## 1. Executive Summary

- **NL/BE stadiums are increasingly marketed as corporate event venues** — The Arena Group operates 18 stadium venues across the Benelux (incl. Stade Charleroi, Lotto Park/Anderlecht) for corporate events, product launches, and team building. This proves the concept works commercially.
- **Den Bosch has zero stadium-based event venues** — The local market (Brabanthallen, 1931 Congrescentrum, De Ruwenberg, Orangerie) is dominated by conventional congress centres, estates, and church conversions. No competitor offers the "iconic stadium wow factor."
- **The caterer-as-hero model works** — Top-performing caterer-venue partnerships succeed when the caterer leads the narrative (tastings, storytelling, curated packages) rather than serving as an interchangeable vendor behind the venue brand.
- **"Wow factor" positioning converts at premium price points** — Non-traditional venues that lead with emotional triggers ("imagine your guests walking onto the pitch") and show versatile configurations outperform venues that lead with square metres and facilities.
- **Budget range of €75–€250/pp is competitive** — This sits above standard congress/hotel catering (€40–€75/pp) and below ultra-luxury private dining (€300+/pp), making it ideal for corporate dinners, launches, and exclusive team events.

## 3. Top 5 Global Examples of Stadiums as Event Venues

### 1. The Arena Group (Belgium/Netherlands)
**Model:** Manages 18 stadium venues as a unified event platform. Markets them for staff parties, team building, product presentations, conferences, concerts, and onboarding events. Proves that stadium-as-venue is a repeatable, scalable business across the Benelux.

### 2. Johan Cruijff ArenA — Amsterdam
**Model:** Markets skyboxes, lounges, and pitch-side areas for corporate dinners and product launches year-round. Leverages Ajax brand recognition. Offers "ArenA tours + dinner" packages that blend experience with premium hospitality.

### 3. Tottenham Hotspur Stadium — London
**Model:** Purpose-built with dedicated banqueting and event floors separate from matchday operations. Hosts fashion shows, product launches, and awards ceremonies. Markets as "London's most versatile venue" — not as a football stadium. Capacity: up to 2,000 for seated dinners.

### 4. Mercedes-Benz Stadium — Atlanta, USA
**Model:** "The stadium reimagined" — positions corporate events as experiences with retractable roof, world-class AV, and pitch-level access. Hosts tech conferences, galas, and exclusive private dinners for 20–2,000 guests.

### 5. Allianz Arena — Munich
**Model:** Year-round event programme including corporate Christmas dinners, team building events, and product launches in the players' lounge and skyboxes. Markets the "behind-the-scenes" experience as the differentiator.

**Key pattern:** Every successful stadium venue leads with the *experience* ("walk where the players walk," "dine pitch-side") rather than the *facilities* (square metres, WiFi, parking).

## 4. Winning Marketing Angles + Example Copy

### Angle 1: The "Wow Factor" Opener
**Why it works:** Non-traditional venues convert when they trigger an emotional "imagine if..." response within the first 3 seconds.

> *"Stel je voor: je gasten stappen het veld op. De spots gaan aan. Dit wordt geen gewoon diner."*
> ("Imagine: your guests step onto the pitch. The lights come on. This is not an ordinary dinner.")

### Angle 2: Versatility Through Visual Proof
**Why it works:** Corporate bookers need to see that the space works for *their* event type. Show 3+ configurations of the same space (dinner, presentation, party).

> *"Eén iconische locatie. Oneindig veel mogelijkheden. Van intiem diner voor 20 tot lancering voor 200."*
> ("One iconic location. Endless possibilities. From intimate dinner for 20 to launch for 200.")

### Angle 3: The Caterer-Led Experience
**Why it works:** When the caterer leads the narrative, the venue becomes the backdrop for a curated experience rather than a rental space with food attached.

> *"Cookaholics × FC Den Bosch Stadion: waar premium catering een iconische locatie ontmoet."*
> ("Cookaholics × FC Den Bosch Stadium: where premium catering meets an iconic location.")

### Angle 4: Exclusivity & Behind-the-Scenes Access
**Why it works:** "Access you can't normally get" is a proven conversion trigger for non-traditional venues.

> *"Dineer waar de spelers lopen. Proost in de skybox. Jouw exclusieve avond in het stadion."*
> ("Dine where the players walk. Toast in the skybox. Your exclusive evening in the stadium.")

### Angle 5: Solving the Event Planner's Problem
**Why it works:** Corporate bookers are stressed, time-poor, and fear boring their guests. Lead with the outcome, not the features.

> *"Op zoek naar een locatie die indruk maakt? Wij regelen alles — van catering tot sfeer. Jij hoeft alleen je gasten uit te nodigen."*
> ("Looking for a venue that impresses? We handle everything — from catering to ambiance. You just invite your guests.")

### Conversion Best Practices (Data-Backed)
- **Virtual tours** remove friction for corporate planners with limited site-visit time (360° + video walkthrough)
- **LinkedIn targeting** of HR professionals and event planners outperforms generic social ads for B2B venue bookings
- **Weekday tiered pricing** (Mon–Thu 20–30% below weekend rates) fills empty stadium dates
- **Pre-event VIP tastings** convert prospects at higher rates than brochures — let them *experience* the combination
- **Bundled all-inclusive packages** simplify procurement for corporate buyers (catering + AV + styling in one price)

## 5. Local Market Gap — Den Bosch / Brabant

### What exists:
- **Large-scale congress facilities** (Brabanthallen, 1931) — functional, professional, but not "wow"
- **Green retreats** (De Ruwenberg) — beautiful but rural, estate-style
- **Historic conversions** (Orangerie, F de Gruyter) — charming but expected
- **Standard hotel meeting rooms** — forgettable

### What's missing:
1. **No stadium-based event venue in Den Bosch or Noord-Brabant.** The Arena Group operates in Belgium and larger NL cities, but has no presence in Brabant. FC Den Bosch would be the *first mover* in this region.
2. **No "experience-first" venue** that leads with emotional impact and social-media-worthy moments. Every competitor leads with capacity, rooms, and green certifications.
3. **No integrated caterer-venue brand.** All local venues work with external or on-site catering as a separate line item. Cookaholics as embedded hospitality partner is unique.
4. **Gap in the €75–€250/pp corporate dinner segment** between standard congress hotel packages (~€40–€75/pp) and exclusive private dining venues (€300+/pp).
5. **No venue specifically targeting product launches, exclusive pop-ups, and team experiences** — the fastest-growing corporate event categories.

### Competitive advantage for Cookaholics × FC Den Bosch:
- Only stadium event venue in Noord-Brabant
- Caterer leads the brand (not just "stadium + catering")
- Price point fills an underserved gap
- "Wow factor" that no congress centre or church conversion can match
- Built-in story: unexpected location = social sharing = organic reach

## 6. Top 3 Recommended Actions for Cookaholics

### Action 1: Build a "Stadium Experience" Landing Page (Not a Venue Rental Page)
**What:** Create a dedicated web presence that positions the stadium as a *curated experience by Cookaholics*, not a space for hire. Lead with emotional copy, video walkthroughs of 3 event configurations (intimate dinner, product launch, team event), and a "Plan Your Experience" CTA.

**Why:** Every competitor in Den Bosch markets square metres and room counts. Lead with "imagine your event here" instead. The Arena Group has proven this works across 18 venues. Virtual tours convert corporate planners who don't have time for site visits.

**How:**
- Hero video: drone shot entering empty stadium → lights on → beautifully set table on the pitch
- 3 package tiers: Intimate (20–40 pax), Premium (40–100 pax), Grand (100–200 pax)
- All-inclusive pricing (catering + styling + AV) — no line-item confusion
- Prominent "Powered by Cookaholics" branding on every touchpoint

### Action 2: Launch a "Proef het Stadion" (Taste the Stadium) Tasting Event Series
**What:** Host quarterly invite-only tasting events for 20–30 local event planners, HR managers, and business owners. They experience the venue + food firsthand. Each event has a different theme (corporate dinner, launch party, team outing).

**Why:** Research shows caterers who let prospects *experience* their offering convert at far higher rates than those relying on brochures. This is how The Arena Group and top caterers fill their pipeline. Behind-the-scenes access is the #1 conversion trigger for non-traditional venues.

**How:**
- Curated invite list via LinkedIn targeting (HR, event planners, business owners in Noord-Brabant)
- Each event demonstrates a different stadium configuration
- Follow-up within 48 hours with personalized proposal
- Capture professional photos/video for social proof

### Action 3: Own the "Iconic Venue" Position on LinkedIn & Local B2B Channels
**What:** Run a targeted LinkedIn content + ad campaign positioning Cookaholics × FC Den Bosch as "the most unexpected event venue in Brabant." Share behind-the-scenes content, event recaps, and client testimonials. Target decision-makers in Noord-Brabant.

**Why:** LinkedIn advertising targeting HR professionals and event planners is the highest-converting channel for B2B venue bookings. Local competitors are invisible on LinkedIn — they rely on venue directories (meetings.nl, eventplanner.net) where they compete on specs, not story.

**How:**
- Weekly LinkedIn posts: behind-the-scenes prep, event highlights, chef stories
- Monthly LinkedIn ad campaign targeting: event planners, HR managers, MDs in Noord-Brabant
- Content formula: 70% visual (video/photo of events), 20% client testimonials, 10% offers
- Drive traffic to the experience landing page with "Book a Tasting" CTA
- List on venue directories (meetings.nl, eventplanner.net) as secondary channel
